Who we are

Our idea started from many years of walking our dogs. Like most good boys, our dogs loved water. We would therefore always find new trails by a water source. Growing up in the Washington, D.C. area, this mostly meant rivers and creeks. Unfortunately, most of these trails contained one thing in common - trash. Each trail that we walked on was littered in plastic and other garbage.

Starting small

There is one trail that our dogs like more than most. A creek located where they grew up in McLean, VA. This creek was the origination of CreekyClean. Afte a while, we couldn't take the trash anymore so we devided to do something about it. We, along with our dogs, went for our ritual hike but this time, threw on our waders, rubber gloves and brought trashbags. Our first haul, we filled 10 industrial sized garbage bags...

Inspiring others

We felt so good about or project that we turned this into part of our routine. Each month, we take a break from our regular hike to get down and dirty in the creek removing trash. We believe that there are many others like us who are walk by the piles of trash each day and wishing that it would get cleaned up. The fact is it won't - unless you do it. Plastics and other litter are only getting worse. We need your help to help reduce the amount of waste in our local communities that go on to impact our waterways on a global scale.

Do your part

We are very passionate about keeping our waterways clean and it all starts with your local creeks. Even the smallest of creeks eventually makes its way to the ocean. Removing even a garbage bag a month prevents that trash from reaching the ocean.
